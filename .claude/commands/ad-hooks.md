# Google Ads Hook Writer — Utility Bill Review

Generate high-converting Google Ads headlines and descriptions across 10 proven copywriting frameworks. Adapted from DTC Meta ad methodology for search intent.

## Product Brief (Pre-loaded)

- **Product**: Utility Bill Review — free AI-powered solar proposal analyzer
- **URL**: https://www.utilitybillreview.com
- **Target Customer**: Colorado homeowners who have received a solar proposal/quote and want independent verification before signing
- **Key Benefits**:
  - Free, independent analysis (not a solar company)
  - Uses real NREL data + actual Xcel Energy rates
  - AI grades your quote against Colorado averages
  - Shows cost-per-watt vs market price
  - 60-second results, zero sales pressure
  - Works with any installer's proposal
- **Pain Points**:
  - Overwhelmed by confusing solar proposals
  - Don't know if the quote is fair or inflated
  - Afraid of overpaying for solar
  - Distrust of solar salespeople
  - Don't understand net metering, tax credits
  - Paralyzed by too many options/installers
  - Worried about high-pressure sales tactics
- **Differentiators**: Not a solar company. No sales pitch. Independent data. Colorado-specific.
- **Current Best Angles**: "Is your quote fair?", "Check before you sign", "No sales pitch, just data"

## Framework Instructions

Generate ad copy across ALL 10 frameworks below. For each framework, produce:
- **2 Headlines** (max 30 characters each — STRICTLY enforced, count every character)
- **2 Descriptions** (max 90 characters each — STRICTLY enforced)
- **Usage note**: When this framework works best

### The 10 Frameworks

1. **Problem-Solution**: Name the pain, present the fix
2. **Curiosity Gap**: Tease what they'll discover, make them click to find out
3. **Bold Claim**: Make a strong, specific, defensible statement
4. **Social Proof**: Leverage numbers, trust signals, authority data sources
5. **Before/After**: Contrast the confused state vs. the clarity they'll get
6. **Us vs Them**: Position against solar companies, sales pitches, generic calculators
7. **Question Hook**: Ask the exact question running through their mind
8. **Contrarian**: Challenge common solar industry assumptions
9. **Urgency/Scarcity**: Time-sensitive angles (rate changes, ITC stepdown, seasonal)
10. **Storytelling**: Mini-narrative that mirrors the customer journey

## Output Format

For each framework, output:

```
### [Framework Name]
**Best for**: [when to use this angle]

Headlines:
1. "[headline]" ([X]/30 chars)
2. "[headline]" ([X]/30 chars)

Descriptions:
1. "[description]" ([X]/90 chars)
2. "[description]" ([X]/90 chars)
```

## Critical Rules

- NEVER write generic hooks like "Go Solar Today" or "Save Money" — every hook must target a SPECIFIC pain point
- ALWAYS count characters precisely — headlines over 30 chars are REJECTED by Google Ads
- ALWAYS write 2 variations per framework (20 headlines + 20 descriptions total)
- Include the framework name and a one-line note on when to use each one
- Write for HIGH COMMERCIAL INTENT — these people already have a solar quote
- Use Colorado-specific references where natural (Xcel Energy, Denver, NREL)
- DO NOT use exclamation marks in headlines (Google Ads policy)
- Headlines should work independently (Google RSA mixes and matches them)
- After generating all hooks, output a **Top 5 Headlines** and **Top 3 Descriptions** recommendation for immediate A/B testing

## After Generation

Once all 20 headline + 20 description pairs are generated:
1. Pick your **Top 5 Headlines** to test first (choose across different frameworks for diversity)
2. Pick your **Top 3 Descriptions** that pair well with any headline
3. Suggest which current campaign headlines should be REPLACED with these new ones
4. Recommend a 2-week A/B testing plan: which hooks to test in Week 1 vs Week 2
